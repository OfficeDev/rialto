How to use the Rialto chat system

What is the deal with the "chat crawl"?

We encourage group discussion of the content being presented, to spark new ideas and help the group as a whole get smarter and better-connected. In the conference room, chat activity on our channel is displayed on screens to either side of the main stage.

You can go to our Teams channel to participate in the discussion.

The system is intended to be a fun way for the audience to participate that (hopefully) does not excessively distract the room's attention and remains respectful of the current speaker on stage.

How does it do that?

It shows a "curated" view of the live chat stream instead of a raw feed:

  • Only top-level posts are shown (not full reply threads).
  • Posts that ask a question are shown above other posts.
  • Posts are ranked by "engagement", currently measured by the number of unique people replying.
  • Replies in comment threads can be marked as an "answer", causing it to show up on the live stream.
  • Text-only (no GIFs).

This works via a Teams bot that listens to all channel activity. It can also accept commands added to the end of replies:

  •  Mark your reply as the answer the original post with /answer
  •  Pin post to the top with /pin (undo with /unpin)
  • Hide post with /hide (undo with /unhide)

Last writer wins. Be civil :)

Can I just "like" a post to increase its engagement score?

Unfortunately, due to technical limitations, the Rialto bot won't get notifications of likes. You "upvote" a post by adding a reply, which could just be "+1". Only the number of unique people replying, not the total number of replies, determines the engagement score for ranking.

Can I edit a post after it's appeared on the big screen?

Similar to "likes", Teams does not send edit notifications to bots, so it will not know about your edit. You can use the /hide command in a reply to remove any post from the screen if necessary. Or use /answer to add a clarification.

What does adding a #hashtag to a post do?

Each presentation has its own hashtag. If you want to associate your post with a presentation that is not currently happening live on stage, add its hashtag. There is no need to add a hashtag if your post is about the current presentation, it will be associated automatically.

You can use the #offtopic hashtag for comments not associated with anything on the agenda.

Each presentation has a permalink (keyed via its hashtag) that allows you to pull up the comments for that talk (i.e. reconstruct the live view that was in the room at the time).

All hashtags are listed in the agenda. Note: if you add multiple hashtags, your post will only be associated with the first one (see exception about #TellMeMore below).

What is special about the #TellMeMore hashtag?

Near the end of the conference, we will have a panel discussion that we'll organize "just in time" based on what we think the audience would be interested in learning more about, and we'll bring a few speakers from earlier in the conference back up on stage.

To help inform whom we select, you can tag any post (including replies) with the special #TellMeMore hashtag (lowercase is fine too). This hashtag can be combined with regular speaker hashtags and the right thing will happen.
